Executive readout

Narrative synthesis — what's happening on the UK back book and why.

The UK back book stands at £34.1M outstanding across 499,923 active loans as of 2026-06-22, with DQ4+ at 5.8% and DQ30+ at 3.2%. Portfolio health is mixed across four lenses: flow metrics show early delinquency entry stabilising (FFR 0→1 2.8%) but persistently weak B1 cure (~51%); stock DQ has risen from a Dec-25 trough of 3.8% to 5.8% as Q1 vintages season; segment concentration in SPI (21.0M, DQ4+ 6.6%) and repeat borrowers (FFR 0→1 5.1%) elevates risk versus Non-SPI and NTA; and economics show lifetime NACO of 1.54% by origination volume with charge-off acceleration in May-26 (£242K, 0.97% monthly CO rate). Prime+ dominates at 82% of balance with 3.4% DQ4+, but Near Prime contributes disproportionate stress at 18.0% DQ4+. Deep dive — Jan / Feb 2026

Diagnostic thread for the worst-performing Q1 capture months flagged in the charts and seasoning table above.

What is driving Jan / Feb 2026 high DQ4+?

Root-cause decomposition for the two worst-performing Q1 capture vintages — links post-holiday pay stress, B1 roll-forward, volume/mix, and repeat concentration to observed DQ build.

Jan-26 (12.0% DQ4+, 8.9% DQ30+) and Feb-26 (13.5% DQ4+, 9.7% DQ30+) are the worst-performing vintages on the remaining book — despite ~98% of Jan and ~97% of Feb origination volume already current or paid off.
DriverMechanismEvidence
1. Post-holiday pay-cycle stress Jan/Feb captures hit 1st/2nd payments during UK's tightest cash-flow window Portfolio FFR 0→1 peaked ~3.5% in Jan-26; Jan DQ4+ rose from 3.7% (Feb) → 12.0% (May)
2. Weak B1 cure (roll-forward) ~50% of B1 loans roll to 30+ DPD; early misses compound into DQ30+ FFR 1→2 ~49% → only ~51% B1 cure; Jan DQ30+ now 8.9%
3. Volume ramp & mix shift Large Q1 origination waves (10.2M Jan, 9.6M Feb) with approval rate ~71% SPI + repeat share growing; SPI DQ4+ 6.6% vs Non-SPI 4.5%
4. Repeat borrower concentration Repeat FFR 0→1 runs 2–3× NTA; <20% cure past 30 DPD Repeat FFR 0→1 5.1% vs NTA 2.3%
5. Still deteriorating (Jan) Jan cohort DQ4+ still climbing week-on-week through May; Dec cohort improving Jan weekly DQ4+ 11.6% → 14.0% (Apr–May); Dec 15.2% → 11.9% over same window

FPDQ4 at origination was ~6.1% for Jan (better than Dec's 7.1%) — risk built in the back book via roll rates, not at first payment alone.

Originations old enough for first-payment delinquency (FPD) to be observed reliably — i.e. the first scheduled payment has come due and enough time has passed to see whether the borrower reached 4+, 7+, 15+, or 30+ DPD on that payment.
UK filter: captures in credit_first_pmt_dq with DT < today − 30 days (aligned to context §2.3 IS_MATURED_FOR_FPDQX_READING). Current book: 399,002 loans · £54.6M origination £.
Why exclude immature loans? Recent captures would dilute FPDQ rates downward — they are still inside their first payment cycle. That is why May-26 FPDQ30 may show “—” while FPDQ4 is available: longer maturity is required for 30+ DPD first-payment reads.
